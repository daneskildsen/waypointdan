Took the rivercat from South Bank to Lone Pine—best public transport decision of the trip so far. The ferry ride alone was worth it, winding west along the Brisbane River past mansions with private jetties and patches of remaining bushland.

Lone Pine is the world's first and largest koala sanctuary. Got there right when it opened and had the place mostly to myself for the first hour. Held a koala (warm, sleepy, surprisingly heavy), fed kangaroos (aggressive beggars, soft fur), and watched a platypus do absolutely nothing for twenty minutes, which I'm told is peak platypus behaviour.

The bird walkthrough aviary was the surprise highlight—lorikeets landing on your arm, a cassowary eyeing me like I owed it money.

Late afternoon I wandered through the adjacent Fig Tree Pocket bushland reserve. The contrast between the manicured sanctuary and the wild scrub just metres away was striking.
