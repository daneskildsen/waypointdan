Landed in Brisbane mid-morning. The air was thick and warm—a proper subtropical welcome. After dropping bags at the hotel in South Bank, I walked the river promenade toward the city centre.

The Kurilpa Bridge caught my eye first—a striking cable-stayed structure for pedestrians and cyclists. Crossing it felt like walking through a sculpture. On the other side, the CBD rose up behind the Botanic Gardens, glass towers mixed in with old sandstone buildings.

Grabbed a flat white at a courtyard cafe tucked behind Charlotte Street. The coffee culture here is serious business. Sat for an hour just watching the city move.

Headed back toward South Bank as the sun started dropping. The Wheel of Brisbane was lit up against the dusk sky, and the Streets Beach was full of families cooling off. It felt surreal to be in shorts and a t-shirt in April.
