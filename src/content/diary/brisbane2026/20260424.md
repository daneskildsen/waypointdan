The main event. Booked the dawn climb up the Story Bridge and it was everything I'd hoped for.

Strapped into harness and clipped to the guide rail just as the sky started lightening. The ascent is a steady walk up the southern cantilever, with the city slowly revealing itself at each level. By the time we reached the summit platform—74 metres above the river—the sun was breaking over the bay.

The view is impossible to capture in photos. The city skyline to the west, the river snaking east toward Moreton Bay, the mountains faint on the horizon. Our guide pointed out the Glass House Mountains, named by Captain Cook, barely visible through the haze.

After the climb—which lasted about two hours total—I walked across the Captain Cook Bridge into Kangaroo Point and found a café right at the base of the cliffs. Sat there with a bacon-and-egg roll and watched rock climbers working the Kangaroo Point cliffs, a bizarrely urban climbing spot right in the middle of the city.

Spent the afternoon at the Queensland Museum and Gallery of Modern Art (GOMA). The Australia design exhibition was genuinely excellent. Dinner was fish and chips on the river at Eat Street Northshore—a container park full of food stalls and live music.
